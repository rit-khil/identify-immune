Tested positive for SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)?
You can make a difference.

If you have tested positive for COVID-19 you may be immune. You could help by donating plasma, participating in clinical research or assisting health workers.

Those who have tested positive may become immune and may be able to:

  • donate plasma via simple protocol
  • participate in clinical research
  • or become a health worker

    How does it work?

    Volunteers who have tested positive for COVID-19 are needed. During a time of need, mobilizing exactly the right volunteers at the right time is hard and slow. Register now to save time and lives.

    Register

    Check your email for specific volunteer opportunities (e.g. donate blood), consent to have your contact information shared with the volunteer coordinator and proceed Privacy

    Volunteer

    Those who are immune and not contagious will be key in helping others. Volunteer where and when you can.

    Why volunteer?

    There are three major ways that volunteers can help.

    • Giving Blood (Convalescent plasma): there is some evidence that antibodies from blood of those who have recovered can help the very ill. Ongoing research may benefit with your blood donation in your area, particuarly in expediting the process to finding a solution.
    • Participate in Research: We need to understand how to identify who is immune but not contagious, and volunteers may be needed to develop tests and diagnostics.
    • Make a difference: Once we understand who is immune and not contagious, those individuals will be instrumental in assisting medical personnel.
    "If this really goes on for months at a time, for 18 months as some people have projected, having health care workers who are immune to the virus will be really, really helpful."
    - Angela Rasmussen, a virologist at Columbia University in New York (source)

    Privacy

    We take the following measures to protect your privacy.

    You will be given the opportunity to opt in or out to each volunteer opportunity.

    • We will contact you regarding each individual volunteer opportunity. Your contact info not be shared with the volunteer organization unless you opt in.
    • You can remove yourself from the volunteer list at any time.
    • The volunteer list will not be made publically available.
    • We only collect relevant data up front. We ask for email and phone to contact you, diagnosis details for eligibility, and address as volunteer opportunities are closely linked to geography.
    • Data is stored in HIPAA secure application, with access control and access logging.
    • Only team members have access to data. For each volunteer opportunity, you can opt in to participate.
    • Reach out to us with questions any time. Email us: hello@identifyimmune.org
