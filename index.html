Volunteer

Those who are immune and not contagious will be key in helping others. Volunteer where and when you can.

Why volunteer?

There are three major ways that volunteers can help.

  • Convalescent plasma: there is some evidence that antibodies from blood of those who have recovered can help the very ill. Research is ongoing and there may be blood needed in your area.
  • Understanding Immunity: we need to understand how to identify who is immune but not contagious, volunteers may be needed to enhance our understanding.
  • Helping others: once we understand who is immune and not contagious, those people may be needed to assist medical personnel.
  • Every hour counts in this pandemic, so it is hard to predict what volunteer efforts will be needed.
"If this really goes on for months at a time, for 18 months as some people have projected, having health care workers who are immune to the virus will be really, really helpful."
- Angela Rasmussen, a virologist at Columbia University in New York (source)

Privacy

We take the following measures to protect your privacy.

The volunteer list will not be made publically available. You will be given the opportunity to opt in or out to each volunteer opportunity.

  • We will contact you regarding each individual volunteer opportunity. Your contact info not be shared with the volunteer organization unless you opt in.
  • You can remove yourself from the volunteer list at any time
  • We only collect relevant data up front. We ask for email and phone to contact you, diagnosis details for eligibility, and address as volunteer opportunities are mostly linked to geography.
  • Reach out to us with questions any time. Email us: hello@identifyimmune.org
